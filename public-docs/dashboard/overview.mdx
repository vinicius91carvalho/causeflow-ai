The CauseFlow dashboard (local OSS: [`http://localhost:3001`](http://localhost:3001) after `./init.sh` from the [monorepo](https://github.com/vinicius91carvalho/causeflow-ai)) is where you manage every aspect of incident investigation — from monitoring active issues to approving remediations and reviewing team activity.

## Home screen

When you sign in, the dashboard home gives you an immediate read on the health of your systems.

### Metrics

Three key metrics appear at the top of the home screen:

- **Active incidents** — The number of incidents currently in progress (open, triaging, investigating, or remediating). A severity breakdown shows how many are critical, high, medium, low, or informational at a glance.
- **MTTR** — Mean time to resolve. The rolling average time from incident creation to resolution, calculated across your closed incidents.
- **Resolution rate** — The percentage of incidents that reached a resolved or closed state, helping you track whether investigations are completing successfully.

### Recent incidents

Below the metrics, the **Recent incidents** list shows your most recently created incidents. Each row displays:

- Incident title
- Severity badge (color-coded: critical in red, high in orange, medium in yellow, low in blue, info in gray)
- Status badge (open, triaging, investigating, awaiting approval, remediating, resolved, closed)
- Source — where the incident originated (a monitoring integration, trigger, chat, or manual creation)
- Time elapsed since creation

Click any row to open the full incident detail view.

## Sidebar navigation

The sidebar is your primary navigation across the platform. It is available on every page.

| Section | What you find there |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Dashboard** | Home screen with metrics, recent incidents, and quick actions |
| **Incidents** | Full incident list — create, filter, and investigate incidents |
| **Integrations** | Connect and manage your monitoring tools, cloud providers, and third-party services |
| **Skills** | Create and manage custom investigation profiles for your specific systems |
| **Team** | Invite members, assign roles, and manage pending invitations |
| **Settings** | Profile, company info, notifications, API keys, and remediation settings |
| **Billing** | View your plan, usage, and manage your subscription |
| **Audit** | Tamper-proof log of every action taken in your tenant |

### Settings > API Keys

Your API keys for authenticating inbound webhooks are managed from **Settings > API Keys**. From this page you can create new keys, view existing key names and prefixes, and revoke keys that are no longer in use.

<Warning>
  An API key's plaintext value is shown only once at creation time. Copy it immediately — it cannot be retrieved after you close the creation dialog. If a key is lost, revoke it and create a new one.
</Warning>
